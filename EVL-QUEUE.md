## Open Items

- [ ] **Finance/F&I active comparison process (part of Full Service $399 - not standalone).** Decided July 16, 2026: this stays bundled inside Full Service, not a new bucket. Advisory currently only *tells* the customer what rate/terms they should qualify for; this process goes further by actually *shopping and comparing* real offers on their behalf. Full 7-step process worked out (BITOP):
  1. **Trigger:** deal reaches "Offer Ready" stage (ties into the dealStage tracker built July 16) or Full Service is purchased
  2. **Collect:** customer's credit profile (score range, income, down payment, trade equity) - `credit-score.html` already captures some of this. **Also ask:** are you currently serving, a veteran, or an immediate family member of someone who is? (simple yes/no) - determines whether USAA and Navy Federal are included in the comparison pool for that customer. If they qualify and one of those offers the best rate, recommend it - the only rule is never recommend an institution the customer can't actually access.
  3. **Shop rates:** pull current offers from 2-3 benchmark lenders for that credit tier - needs an internal reference sheet built and kept current (not a live API integration, at least initially). **Display rule confirmed:** show only 2-3 lenders per comparison, tailored to that specific customer - if they qualify for USAA/Navy Federal and it wins, show it; if they don't qualify, it's not shown at all (not greyed out, simply absent) - never display an option the customer can't actually access. **Real Experian tier table confirmed (July 16, 2026) - update cadence is QUARTERLY, not every 30 days (corrected from Tim's recollection):** Experian releases new data ~6 weeks after each quarter ends, so Q2 2026 data won't be available until mid-August 2026. Current Q1 2026 table (source: Experian State of the Automotive Finance Market, VantageScore 4.0):

     | Credit Tier | New Car APR | Used Car APR |
     |---|---|---|
     | Super prime (781+) | 4.55% | 6.30% |
     | Prime (661-780) | 6.23% | 8.77% |
     | Near prime (601-660) | 9.67% | 14.03% |
     | Subprime (501-600) | 13.44% | 19.42% |
     | Deep subprime (300-500) | 16.01% | 21.77% |

     **DONE (July 16, 2026):** the credit tier rate table WAS actually already built into `my-buying-power.html` Step 3/4 (correcting an earlier incorrect claim in this log that it didn't exist at all - it was found via a more thorough search) - just with stale rates. Fixed by updating both the displayed tier labels AND the actual `RATES` calculation object to the current Q1 2026 numbers above. Also found: a rate-vs-rebate comparison feature already exists (`incentiveAmount` field + `showRateRebate()` function), but the manufacturer rate is calculated as a hardcoded `qualifying rate minus 2 points` assumption, not real advertised manufacturer rates with honest disclosure language.

     **Still not built:** (1) the "I have my own rate" customer override box (customer types in a rate they were already quoted and sees their real payment, instead of being forced to use the tier estimate), (2) proper manufacturer 0% rate handling with the honest disclosure language discussed previously ("Special financing rates may be available on select models with approved credit through participating lenders...") instead of the current hardcoded -2% guess.
  4. **Compare:** stack those against whatever the dealer's finance office actually quotes at the table
  5. **Present:** side-by-side comparison ("us vs. them") showing 2-4 finance sources against the dealer's offer. **Rate-sourcing confirmed (July 16, 2026):** do NOT require the customer to fill out multiple applications to comparison shop - customers won't do that. Instead, use each lender's publicly advertised rate as the initial hook, clearly labeled as a starting rate (not a personalized quote), since real personalized rates require either a soft-pull prequalification (only PenFed confirmed to offer this among current list - Alliant unclear, needs verification) or a hard-pull full application (USAA and Navy Federal both explicitly do NOT offer prequalification - hard inquiry required to see real rates from either). Customer only does one real application once they've chosen to move forward, not multiple applications to shop around. **Marketing language locked in (capability-framed, not track-record-framed, since no real EVL case data exists yet):** "We shop your rate against multiple qualified lenders to find you the best terms available - potential savings of 1-2% aren't unusual when a dealer's finance office isn't the only option on the table." Swap to a real-numbers version once actual case data exists. Same comparison format and same capability-framed (not track-record) language applies to Step 6 (GAP/warranty) below - don't overstate savings claims there either before real data exists. **Non-disclosure rule confirmed (July 16, 2026) - same principle as the WS Exchange bidding-dealer-name rule:** the actual lender reference sheet (real current advertised rates per lender) lives on an ADMIN-ONLY page, never customer-facing. The customer only ever sees the resulting rate/number ("EVL found you a better rate: X% vs. the dealer's Y%") with zero lender name attached, so they can't just go straight to that lender themselves and cut EVL out. Lender identity is only revealed after the customer commits (pays for Full Service or equivalent).
  6. **Repeat for F&I:** same comparison exercise, scoped to products with real, well-established shopping value (confirmed via research July 16, 2026) - **compare these:** Extended Warranty/VSC (dealer markup typically 50-200% over direct providers like Endurance/Autopom/credit union warranty products), GAP Insurance, Tire & Wheel/Road Hazard, Key Replacement Coverage, Excess Wear & Tear (lease customers only). **Do NOT build comparison-shopping for these - advise customers to just skip them outright, regardless of price, since they're low-value everywhere:** paint/fabric protection, VIN etching, nitrogen tire fill, credit life/disability insurance, windshield replacement coverage, "total loss protection" (redundant if GAP already purchased). EV-specific battery/VSC coverage ("Battery4Life"-style) is an emerging category worth adding to the shop-worthy list as EV volume grows. **Useful stat for customer messaging:** ~75% of buyers who decline these products at the dealership desk and revisit the purchase at home end up paying 40-60% less for the same product elsewhere - validates EVL's whole approach here.
  7. **Document:** capture actual savings achieved - ties into the existing Deal Gap tool (`deal-gap.html`) and doubles as proof-of-value for future customers

  **Lender reference list started (July 16, 2026):** USAA (military/family only - real gate), Navy Federal (military/DoD/family only - real gate), PenFed (open to everyone despite the name - $5 deposit to join), Alliant Credit Union (open to everyone, small donation if needed), RBFCU/Randolph-Brooks (technically qualified-membership but broad enough - includes Dallas/Collin/Denton/Tarrant counties - to be effectively open for EVL's DFW customer base), Capital One / Chase / Bank of America / LightStream (open to everyone, no credit union membership required). List not final - more lenders can be added as decided.

  **Compliance guardrails to maintain (important):** EVL must stay in "we compared and told you" territory, not "we originated your loan" - the customer applies and signs directly with whichever lender/provider wins. If EVL ever takes a referral fee/commission from a lender or F&I provider, that likely triggers licensing requirements (loan broker license for financing; potentially an insurance producer license in Texas for GAP/warranty products specifically, depending on structure). Staying fee-for-advisory (customer pays EVL directly, not paid by the lender) keeps this clean and consistent with EVL's existing non-broker, non-lender positioning.
