⚠️ Don't go into the finance office yet! Complete this checklist first. Once you sign, it's much harder to go back.
📄 What You Should Have With You
All keys to the trade-in vehicle
Both key fobs, valet key, any spare keys. Dealers will reduce your trade value if keys are missing.
Title to your trade-in (if you own it outright)
No title = no trade. If your title has a lien, you'll need your payoff information instead.
All signers are present
Every person on the loan or title must be physically present with a valid ID to sign. No exceptions.
Valid driver's license for ALL signers
Must be current and not expired. Finance office will copy it for the deal jacket.
Proof of insurance
You cannot legally drive your new vehicle off the lot without it. Have your insurance card or app ready.
🚙 Clear Your Trade-In Vehicle
Remove all personal items from glove box
Registration, insurance cards, sunglasses, medications, pens, manuals — check every compartment.
Clear center console completely
Chargers, change, receipts, sunscreen, lip balm, personal items. People forget this one constantly.
Remove garage door openers from sun visor
This is the #1 most forgotten item. Check above both visors. These give access to your home.
Remove toll tags (TxTag, EZPass, etc.)
If left in the trade, the new owner will run up tolls on YOUR account. Peel it off the windshield.
Remove parking passes and hang tags
Work parking passes, HOA hang tags, school permits — check the mirror and visor.
Clear all personal items from trunk
Jumper cables, umbrellas, sports equipment, groceries, tools — do a full trunk sweep.
Remove car seats and child safety items
Car seats, booster seats, strollers in the trunk. These are expensive and easy to forget under stress.
📱 Wipe Electronic Devices in Trade-In
Delete home & work addresses from navigation
Your saved addresses tell strangers exactly where you live and work. Clear your favorites and history.
📖 How to do this on my vehicle
Unpair all Bluetooth devices
Your phone contact list and call history may be stored in the vehicle's system. Delete all paired devices.
📖 How to unpair Bluetooth on my vehicle
Clear garage door programming from HomeLink
Many vehicles have HomeLink buttons on the mirror or overhead console. Clear all programmed doors.
📖 How to clear HomeLink on my vehicle
Cancel or transfer SiriusXM subscription
Call 1-866-635-2349 or go to siriusxm.com to transfer your subscription to your new vehicle or cancel.
Remove dashcam and SD card
If you have a dashcam, remove both the device and the memory card. Your footage is on there.
🔍 Inspect Your New Vehicle Before Signing
Walk completely around the vehicle
Check all four panels, bumpers, roof, and glass for any dings, scratches, or damage. Do this in good lighting.
Confirm all negotiated equipment is on the vehicle
Sunroof, tow package, running boards, floor mats, cargo liner — verify every item you negotiated is actually there.
Test all major features
Sunroof, heated seats, blind spot monitoring, backup camera, remote start. Test before you sign.
Check fuel level
New vehicle should have a full tank. If not, ask the dealership to fill it before delivery.
Verify spare tire is present
Check under the trunk floor or under the vehicle. Some vehicles have a tire inflator kit instead — confirm which one.
⚠️ Don't leave yet! Once you drive off the lot, it's very hard to come back for missing items. Take 5 minutes to complete this checklist.
📋 Documents — Take Everything With You
Buyer's order / purchase agreement
Your complete deal summary showing all numbers. This is your most important document. Do not leave without it.
All finance/loan documents
Your retail installment contract showing your rate, term, payment, and lender. Keep this in a safe place at home.
⚠️ WARRANTY BOOKLETS AND BROCHURES
You paid potentially thousands of dollars for extended warranty, GAP, tire & wheel, and paint protection. Most customers leave without these documents and have NO IDEA what their coverage is. Demand every booklet before you leave.
GAP insurance documents (if purchased)
Shows your GAP provider, coverage amount, and how to file a claim if your vehicle is totaled.
Tire & wheel protection documents (if purchased)
Shows what's covered, the provider, and how to make a claim. Keep in your glove box.
Paint/fabric protection documents (if purchased)
Shows application warranty and re-application schedule. Often has a certificate you must keep.
Temporary tag / paper plate
Must be displayed in the rear window. Confirm expiration date — usually 30-60 days to get your permanent plates.
Title application paperwork
Your copy of the title application. The title itself will be mailed to you or your lender.
🔑 Keys & Physical Items
ALL keys and key fobs for the new vehicle
Most new vehicles come with 2 key fobs. Verify the count and test each one before leaving.
Owner's manual and supplemental booklets
Usually in a pouch in the glove box. Includes the factory warranty booklet — very important to keep.
Floor mats (if purchased or included)
Check front and rear. If you paid for upgraded mats, make sure they're installed in the vehicle.
License plate from trade-in vehicle
In Texas, plates belong to the owner — not the vehicle. Remove your plate before leaving the trade.
Salesperson's direct contact card
Get their cell number, not just the dealership's main line. You'll need it for follow-up issues.
✅ Last Steps Before You Drive Away
Connect your phone to Bluetooth
Do this now while the salesperson is available to help. Don't figure it out alone on the highway.
📖 How to pair Bluetooth on my new vehicle
Set up navigation with your home address
Program your home address while you're still at the dealership. Ask the salesperson to help if needed.
Adjust seat, mirrors, and steering wheel
Take a few minutes to get comfortable before driving. Many new vehicles have power adjustments — learn them now.
Take photos of the vehicle condition
Walk around and photograph any pre-existing marks or scratches. Protects you from being blamed later.
Transfer your toll tag to the new vehicle
Move your TxTag or EZPass to the new windshield. Update your account with the new license plate number.
