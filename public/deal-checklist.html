Review this checklist 24 hours before your appointment. Arriving prepared means your delivery goes smoothly — no delays, no surprises, no return trips.
📄 Documents to Bring
Valid driver's license — ALL signers
Every person signing the loan or title must bring a current, non-expired photo ID. No exceptions — the finance office will copy it for the deal jacket.
Proof of insurance
You cannot legally drive your new vehicle off the lot without it. Full coverage is required if you are financing. Have your insurance card or app ready. Call your agent before you arrive to add the new vehicle.
Payment — certified check, cashier's check, or credit union draft
If paying cash or making a down payment, bring a certified check or cashier's check from your bank or credit union. Personal checks may cause delays. Confirm with the dealership what forms of payment they accept.
All signers must be physically present
If two people are on the loan or title, both must be present with valid ID to sign. If a co-signer is in another state, contact EVL immediately — there are specific steps required.
🔄 Trade-In Preparation — Do This Before You Leave Home
Bring ALL keys to the trade-in vehicle
Both key fobs, the valet key, and any spare keys. Missing keys reduce your trade-in value. Dealers use this as a negotiating tool against you.
Bring the title to your trade-in (if you own it outright)
No title means no trade. If your vehicle is paid off and you don't have the title, contact your DMV to request a duplicate before your appointment.
Know your payoff amount (if you still owe on the trade)
Call your lender before your appointment. Payoff amounts are only good for 10-20 days due to the lag in receiving the title from the state. Get the exact 10-day payoff figure.
Remove all personal items from the trade-in NOW
Do this before you leave home — not in the dealership parking lot. Check every compartment thoroughly.
Remove garage door openers from sun visor
This is the #1 most forgotten item. These give strangers access to your home. Check above BOTH visors before leaving.
Remove toll tags — TxTag, EZPass, or similar
If left in the trade, the new owner will run up tolls on YOUR account. Peel it off the windshield before you arrive.
Clear all electronics in the trade-in
Delete your home address from navigation, unpair your phone from Bluetooth, clear HomeLink garage programming, and cancel or transfer your SiriusXM subscription.

⚠️ EVL Pro Tip — The Key Situation

Many new vehicles today are delivered with only ONE key fob — even on $50,000+ purchases. Dealers rarely mention this upfront. A replacement key can cost $350–$500 after the fact.

Ask this question before you sign anything:

"Before we finalize the deal, can you confirm how many keys this vehicle comes with? If it's only one, I'd like a second key included in the purchase price."
⚠️ Complete this BEFORE you go into the finance office. Once you sign, it is very difficult to address vehicle condition issues. Walk through every item below with your salesperson right now.
🏎️ Test Drive — The Exact Vehicle You Are Buying
I test drove THIS exact vehicle — not a demo or similar model
Always insist on driving the actual vehicle you are purchasing. A demo may drive differently. This is your right as a buyer.
No unusual sounds, vibrations, or warning lights during the test drive
Any noise, shimmy, pull, or warning light should be addressed BEFORE signing. Document anything unusual now.
Brakes feel firm and responsive
Spongy brakes, grinding, or pulling to one side are red flags. Test at various speeds.
AC and heat both work properly
Test both systems regardless of the weather outside. Air conditioning issues are expensive to repair.
🔍 Exterior Walk-Around — Do This in Good Lighting
Walked completely around the vehicle — all four sides
Start at the driver's front corner and walk clockwise. Check every panel, bumper, roof, and glass surface. Take photos of any marks you find.
All glass is free of chips, cracks, or damage
Windshield, rear window, all side windows, and sunroof if equipped. Even a small chip can spread into a full crack.
All four tires in good condition — no damage or uneven wear
Check tread depth and look for sidewall damage, bulges, or bubbles. Verify all four tires match in brand and size.
VIN on the dashboard matches ALL paperwork
Look through the windshield at the dashboard plate. This 17-digit number must match your buyer's order, title application, and loan documents exactly.
Monroney/window sticker present and matches what was negotiated (new vehicles)
The window sticker is a federal requirement. It shows MSRP, all options, and fuel economy. Verify everything you negotiated is listed. For used vehicles — confirm you received the Buyers Guide showing warranty status.
🪑 Interior Inspection
All seats, carpet, and headliner are free of tears, stains, or damage
Check every seat including the rear. Look at the headliner for sagging or stains. Check carpet under the floor mats.
All controls, buttons, and switches work correctly
Test every button on the dashboard, steering wheel, and doors. Power windows, locks, mirrors — test them all.
All seatbelts retract and latch properly
Check every seating position. A seatbelt that doesn't retract or latch is a safety issue and a warranty claim.
No warning lights on after the engine starts
Start the engine and wait 30 seconds. All warning lights should go off. Any light that stays on must be explained by the dealer before you sign.
📱 Technology & Features Verification
Infotainment screen powers on and responds correctly
Touch the screen in multiple spots. Test the volume, navigation if equipped, and radio. Ask the salesperson to walk you through the basics now.
Backup camera displays clearly
Put the vehicle in reverse and verify the camera shows a clear image. Check that the guidelines display correctly.
Driver assistance features active — blind spot, lane keep, forward collision
If you paid for these features verify they are present and active. Have the salesperson demonstrate each one.
Remote start works (if equipped)
Test the remote start from the key fob before leaving the lot. Remote start issues are a common delivery complaint.
Heated and cooled seats work (if equipped)
Turn them on and verify you feel heat or cooling within 60 seconds. These are expensive to repair after the fact.
🔧 Spare Tire — Know What You Have Before You Need It
⚠️ Important: Many new vehicles no longer include a spare tire. Instead they come with a tire inflation canister — a small compressor and sealant that only works on minor punctures. This surprises most customers when they get their first flat. Know what your vehicle has RIGHT NOW.
Confirmed whether this vehicle has a spare tire OR an inflation canister
Ask your salesperson right now. Check under the trunk floor or under the vehicle. If it's a canister — ask if a spare tire can be added to the deal.
Jack and lug wrench are present (if spare tire equipped)
Used vehicles especially — the spare, jack, and lug wrench should all be in their proper storage location. Check now.
🔑 Key Count — Verify Before You Sign
Counted and tested ALL keys — know exactly how many you are receiving
Most new vehicles come with 2 key fobs. CPO vehicles should come with 2 as part of certification standards. Used vehicles vary. Test each key fob — lock, unlock, panic button, and remote start if applicable. If you're only getting 1 key — negotiate a second key NOW before you go into the finance office.
📝 We-Owe Items — Document Everything Now
What is a We-Owe? If anything was promised to you that isn't on the vehicle or ready today — floor mats, window tint, a repair, an accessory — it must be documented on a We-Owe form BEFORE you go into the finance office. You get a copy. The deal jacket gets a copy. The service department gets a copy. This is your legal protection.
All promised items are on the vehicle OR documented on a We-Owe form
Floor mats, tint, accessories, repairs — if it was promised and it's not there, get it in writing right now. Verbal promises made in the showroom disappear after you sign.
I have my copy of the We-Owe form (if applicable)
The We-Owe form is created in triplicate. Your copy is your legal guarantee. Do not leave without it if any items are owed to you.
I know who will contact me and when to schedule We-Owe items
Before you leave, get the name and direct number of the service advisor who will handle your We-Owe items. Ask: "When can I expect to hear from someone, and what should I do if I don't hear back within 5 business days?"
✅ Certified Pre-Owned — If Applicable
Received a copy of the CPO inspection checklist
Every CPO vehicle goes through a multi-point inspection. You have the right to see what was inspected and what passed. Ask for this document — it belongs to you.
CPO vehicle came with 2 keys as required by certification standards
Most manufacturer CPO programs require 2 keys as part of certification. If you only received 1, ask the dealer to provide the second before delivery.
✍️ Dealer Delivery Form — Both Parties Sign
Dealer delivery form completed — VIN, mileage, and condition documented
This form records the vehicle's condition, mileage, and key count at the exact moment of delivery. Both you and the salesperson sign it. You get a copy. This is your protection if any dispute arises later.

Buyer's order / purchase agreement

⚠️ WARRANTY BOOKLETS — The Most Forgotten Documents
You may have paid thousands of dollars for an extended warranty, GAP insurance, tire and wheel protection, and paint or fabric protection. Most customers leave without these booklets and have no idea what their coverage is or how to file a claim. Demand every single warranty booklet before you leave. Open your glove box right now and count them.
GAP insurance documents (if purchased)
Shows your GAP provider, policy number, coverage amount, and exactly how to file a claim if your vehicle is totaled. This document is critical — without it you may not know your coverage was ever purchased.
Tire and wheel protection documents (if purchased)
Shows the provider, what is covered, and how to make a claim. Store this in your glove box — you'll need it when you hit a pothole.
Owner's manual and all supplemental booklets
Usually in a pouch in the glove box. The factory warranty booklet is inside — keep it. It explains exactly what Toyota, Honda, Ford, or your manufacturer will cover and for how long.
My copy of the We-Owe form (if any items are owed)
This is your legal guarantee for anything still owed to you. Keep it somewhere you can find it easily.
Temporary tag / paper plate confirmed and displayed
In Texas, the temporary tag is affixed to the rear license plate holder. Verify it is secured before leaving. Your permanent steel plate will come later — confirm with the dealer whether you pick it up or it will be mailed.
🔑 Keys and Physical Items
Counted ALL keys — they are all in my possession right now
Do a final count. All key fobs should be in your hand before you start the car to leave.
Floor mats installed in the vehicle (if purchased or included)
Check front and rear. If you paid for upgraded mats, verify they are the correct ones for your vehicle.
License plate removed from trade-in vehicle
In Texas plates belong to the owner not the vehicle. If you traded in a vehicle, confirm you removed your plate before it left the lot.
Salesperson's direct contact information
Get their personal cell number — not just the dealership main line. You will need it for follow-up on We-Owe items or questions.
🔧 Service Department Introduction
Met a service advisor by name before leaving
Best practice is for your salesperson to walk you into the service department and introduce you to a specific service advisor by name. This builds a relationship and ensures your We-Owe items and future maintenance are handled by someone who knows you.
Understand what your factory maintenance plan covers
If you purchased a Toyota — ToyotaCare covers 2 years or 25,000 miles of scheduled maintenance including oil changes and tire rotations at no cost. Oil changes are at 10,000 mile intervals — not every 5,000 miles as many customers assume. ToyotaCare Plus extends this to 5 years or 75,000 miles for an additional cost. Ask your service advisor about your specific plan before you leave.
