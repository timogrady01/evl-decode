🔑 Collateral & Ownership

You own the vehicle. The lender has a "lien" (a legal claim) on the title until you pay off the loan. You can drive it, modify it (mostly), and sell it — but you must pay the lender off first. Once you pay the last payment, the lender releases the lien and you get the clear title.

If you wreck it: Insurance pays the lender first (up to the loan balance), then you get any remainder. This is why GAP insurance is essential — it covers the "gap" between what insurance pays and what you still owe.
