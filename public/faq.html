What is a lease return vehicle and why should I consider one? ▼
A lease return is a vehicle coming off a 2-3 year lease. They're typically:
  • ✅ 2-4 years old with under 36,000 miles
  • ✅ Maintained to factory standards (required by lease)
  • ✅ Already inspected and graded

What is a lease transfer and how does it work? ▼
A lease transfer (also called a lease swap or lease assumption) is when you take over someone else's existing lease. Here's why it can be a great deal:
  • 🔄 The original lessee needs out early — job transfer, life change, etc.
  • 💰 They often offer incentives — first month paid, cash bonus
  • 📅 You get a shorter remaining term — lower risk commitment
  • ✅ Payment and vehicle are already established — no negotiating needed
  • 🚘 You can get into a luxury vehicle at a great monthly rate
Example: Someone is 12 months into a 36-month BMW X5 lease at $689/mo. They need out and offer you $500 cash + first month free. You take over 24 remaining months at $689/mo.

EVL sources these through platforms like SwapALease and LeaseTrader, as well as our own dealer network.
💡 Early termination penalties can run $2,000-$10,000. Sellers are motivated to offer good incentives to avoid those fees — which means great deals for buyers.

📋 Leasing 8
Should I lease or buy? What's the difference? ▼
Lease = pay for the depreciation. Buy = pay for the whole vehicle.

Leasing makes sense if you:
  • ✅ Want lower monthly payments ($100-$150 less/mo on average)
  • ✅ Like driving a new vehicle every 2-3 years
  • ✅ Don't want to worry about long-term maintenance
  • ✅ Are a business owner (lease payments can be tax deductible)
  • ✅ Want to avoid the "equity trap" of financing a depreciating asset
Buying makes sense if you:
  • ✅ Drive significantly more than 12,000-15,000 miles/year
  • ✅ Want to own the vehicle long-term (7+ years)
  • ✅ Want to customize or modify the vehicle
  • ✅ Prefer no mileage restrictions
💡 EVL helps you analyze both options with real numbers — not a dealership's spin.
What is a money factor and residual value? ▼
These two numbers determine 90% of your lease payment:

Money Factor = the lease's interest rate in disguise. Multiply by 2,400 to convert to APR. (.00089 × 2400 = 2.14% APR). Lower is better for you.

Residual Value = what the manufacturer says the car will be worth at lease end, expressed as a percentage of MSRP. (52% means a $50,000 car has a $26,000 residual). Higher residual = lower payment.

💡 Most customers never know these numbers. Dealers rarely volunteer them. EVL always shows you both — so you know exactly what you're paying and why.
What about mileage limits on a lease? ▼
Standard leases allow 10,000-15,000 miles per year. Going over typically costs $0.15-$0.25 per mile.

EVL's approach:
  • We calculate your actual annual mileage before structuring the lease
  • We build in appropriate mileage upfront — it's cheaper than overage fees
  • If you drive over 15,000 miles/year, we'll tell you leasing may not be ideal
💡 Never guess on mileage. A 12,000-mile lease on a 15,000-mile driver costs thousands in penalties. EVL structures it right from the start.
What are my options at the end of a lease? ▼
At lease end you have four options:

1. Return the vehicle — walk away, no further obligation (assuming normal wear and under mileage)

2. Buy it out — purchase at the predetermined residual value. If market value is above residual, you have equity — that's money in your pocket

3. Trade up — roll into a new lease, often with loyalty incentives from the manufacturer

4. Lease transfer — if you need out early, EVL can find someone to take over your lease

⚡ EVL monitors your lease 90-60-30 days before maturity and contacts you with a full analysis of which option makes the most financial sense at that exact moment.

Three options for early exit:

2. Lease Buyout + Sale — If market value exceeds your residual, buy the vehicle at residual and sell it for a profit.

3. Early Termination — Return the vehicle and pay the penalty. Last resort — most expensive option.

Are there tax advantages to leasing for my business? ▼
Yes — leasing offers significant tax advantages for business owners:
  • 💰 Lease payments are generally fully deductible as a business expense
  • 📊 No depreciation schedule required (unlike purchased vehicles)
  • ✅ Sales tax paid monthly (vs. all at once on purchase)
  • 🚗 Fleet leasing allows predictable monthly expense budgeting
⚠️ EVL is not a tax advisor. Consult your CPA regarding your specific situation. These are general guidelines only.
How do manufacturer lease specials and incentive programs work? ▼
Manufacturers change their lease programs every month. They use lease incentives to:
  • Move slow-selling models (subvented money factor = lower rate)
  • Conquest competitors' customers (cash for switching brands)
  • Retain loyal customers (loyalty cash on renewal)
  • Clear out model year changeovers
⚡ EVL's Auto Alert system monitors manufacturer programs monthly and notifies you when a great deal appears on your target vehicle. This is how you get the timing right.

What credit score do I need? ▼
Credit requirements vary by lender and vehicle type. General guidelines:
  • 🟢 720+ — Tier 1 rates, best available terms
  • 🟡 660-719 — Good rates, minor premium
  • 🟠 600-659 — Approved with higher rate, larger down payment may be required
  • 🔴 Below 600 — Special financing available, limited options
EVL works with lenders across all credit tiers. We'll always tell you what your options are — honestly, not what you want to hear.
Can I use my own bank or credit union financing? ▼
Absolutely — and we encourage it. If you have a pre-approval from your credit union or bank at a great rate, bring it. EVL will:

  • ✅ Use your pre-approved financing as leverage
  • ✅ Compare it against all available options
  • ✅ Tell you honestly which is the better deal
Credit unions typically offer the lowest rates in the market. If you're a credit union member, check their auto loan rate before anything else.
How much down payment do I need? ▼
Down payment requirements vary by credit tier and vehicle type:

  • 💰 $0 down — available for Tier 1 credit on most new vehicles
  • 💰 10-20% — standard for used vehicles or Tier 2 credit
  • 💰 Trade-in equity — can substitute for cash down payment
💡 On a lease, putting money down is generally not recommended — if the vehicle is totaled, you lose that cash. Keep it in your pocket and pay monthly.
Do I need GAP insurance? ▼
GAP (Guaranteed Asset Protection) insurance is highly recommended if you're financing or leasing, especially with little or no down payment.

It covers the "gap" between what you owe and what your vehicle is worth if it's totaled. Without it, you could owe thousands after an accident even though the vehicle is gone.
