In Case of Accident
Part of every Service Vault — the information you need, exactly when you need it most.
🚨 1. Emergency — At The Scene
📞 Call 911
Do Not Admit Guilt
  1. Call 911 if anyone is injured or the vehicles are blocking traffic.
  2. If your vehicle is drivable and it's safe to do so, move it out of the flow of traffic.
  3. If it's not safe to move your vehicle, stay inside with your seatbelt on until help arrives — do not stand in a traffic lane.
  4. Turn on hazard lights and set out flares or triangles if you have them and it's safe to do so.
  5. Exchange insurance information, license plate, and government-issued ID (driver's license, passport, etc.) with the other driver(s).
  6. Photograph the damage to all vehicles and the overall scene.
  7. Contact your Next of Kin using the saved info in Drawer 4 below.
  8. Request a copy of the police accident report — ask the responding officer how and where to obtain it (most departments provide this online or through their records division within a few business days).

🗣️ 7. What To Say — Word Tracks & Q&A
Calling Your Dealership
"Hi, this is [Name]. I purchased my [vehicle] with you on [date], and I was just in an accident. I wanted to let you know in case my insurance or body shop needs to verify anything, like service records or the original invoice. Do you have a service department that handles collision repairs, or can you recommend one?"
Questions to ask:
• Do you offer a loaner vehicle during repair?
• Can I get a copy of my purchase or service records for the claim?
• Will this accident affect my factory warranty coverage?
Calling Your Insurance Company
"Hi, I need to file a claim. My policy number is [X]. I was in an accident on [date] at [location]. [Brief description of what happened]. No one was injured / [describe injuries]. I have photos of the damage and the other driver's insurance information."
Questions to ask:
• Is a rental car covered while my vehicle is being repaired, and for how long?
• Will you use OEM (factory) or aftermarket parts for this repair?
• What's my deductible for this claim?
• How long does the claims process typically take?
• Can I choose my own body shop, or do I need to use one from your network?
Calling The Body Shop
"Hi, I need an estimate for repair on my [vehicle]. My insurance company is [X] and my claim number is [Y]. Can you give me a written estimate of the cost and expected timeline before you start any work?"
Questions to ask:
• Will you use OEM (factory) or aftermarket parts?
• Do you offer a warranty on the repair work?
• What's the expected timeline for completion?
• Will you update me on repair status, or do I need to follow up myself?
• Is a loaner or rental car available through your shop?
