⚖️ Legal Framework — No Dealer License Required

Last updated: Jun 12, 2026 — Confidential. Internal use only. Not legal advice.

This section documents the legal and business model foundation that confirms EVL does not require a dealer license to operate — supported by industry precedent, Texas law framework, and direct competitive validation.

① The Core Legal Position
  • ✓ EVL is a technology and advisory platform — not a dealer. EVL never takes title to any vehicle at any point in any transaction.
  • ✓ EVL never holds inventory, never purchases vehicles for resale, and never acts as a principal in any buy/sell transaction.
  • ✓ EVL coordinates, facilitates, and earns service fees — the same model as a real estate broker, an escrow company, or a technology marketplace.
  • ✓ In Texas, a dealer license is required only when buying and selling vehicles in your own name for profit — taking title, holding inventory, and reselling. EVL does none of these.
  • ✓ Every EVL page carries the disclaimer: "EVL is a vehicle advisory platform — not a dealer." This is not just positioning — it is the legal foundation of the business model.

MINIMUM CONDITION STANDARD (ONE RULE SET, NATIONWIDE)

Windshield: any crack/chip inside the wiper-swept area = flagged. Outside that area, chips under ~1" = acceptable.
Lenses (head/tail/brake/turn): must be uncracked and intact. Light color must match position (red rear/brake, amber turn, white front) — clear housings with correctly-colored LEDs are fine.
Dash: photo with engine running; any illuminated warning light flagged/disclosed on BOL, doesn't auto-fail.
Conservative enough to pass inspection in the strictest state in the network — removes "well my state allows it" disputes entirely.

⚠️ WIRE FRAUD MITIGATION — BUILD FROM DAY ONE

BD and ID often meet for the first time via EVL — classic Business Email Compromise target (fraudulent wire instructions sent via intercepted email). Mitigation: EVL displays each dealer's verified banking/wire info directly on the platform (captured at onboarding), so BD's business office confirms wire details against EVL's record — never solely from an emailed packet.
